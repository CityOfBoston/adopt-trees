The Urban Forest

Increasing Boston's urban forest will benefit city residents through environmental and aesthetic improvements. City trees enrich the lives of all residents by providing many benefits, including: beautification of city streets, providing a respite on hot summer days, improved air and water quality, and offsetting our carbon emissions.

Greenovate Boston and the Grow Boston Greener campaign aim to plant 100,000 trees by 2020 with the overarching goal of increasing Boston's tree canopy from 29% to 35% by 2030. The city is planting over 1,000 trees a year to meet this goal!

Newly planted trees have a difficult time getting established in the tough urban environment. Often these young trees die within a few seasons and a new tree is planted in their place. If this cycle continues it will be difficult for the city to reach its goal of increasing the tree canopy to 35%. With the help of individuals, businesses, and organizations, young trees have a better chance of survival. Continue reading to learn how YOU can help Boston's urban forest grow!

Tree Care Tips

By helping to water and care for a young street tree, you can help establish a healthy tree canopy on your street. Some quick tips are listed below, but be sure to download the tree care guide for more detailed advice.

Water Weekly – Newly planted trees should be watered from May to October. They need 10 gallons once a week and twice as much in hot months like July and August.

Tree Pit Care – Tree pits need to be cleared of trash, the top three inches of soil should be loosened, mulch will help keep the tree roots moist, and a border will help to protect them.

Helpful Tools – Shovel, soil cultivator, gardening gloves, hose or watering can (THIS CAN carries 6 gallons and has a spout to pour in the treegator).

Workshops – Tree care workshops will be offered in the spring and fall through the Boston Natural Areas Network.
